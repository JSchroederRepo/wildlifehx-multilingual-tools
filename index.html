How English & language names are chosen

American English uses iNaturalist's preferred English name (for birds this generally follows the Clements/eBird-style name). iNaturalist does not tag US vs UK English; “British English (iNat alternates)” lists the other distinct English names it carries (e.g. Great Northern Diver) and may include alternates that are not specifically British — treat it as a cue to double-check. Other languages come from iNaturalist's vernacular-name records by locale. If the wrong species appears, pick another match above.

Taxon groups (tick to include):

“Plankton” is a heuristic bucket: it covers curated planktonic groups (copepods, krill, cladocerans, chaetognaths, larvaceans, salps, ctenophores, siphonophores, foraminifera, radiolarians, tintinnids, diatoms, dinoflagellates, coccolithophores, haptophytes) matched against each taxon’s ancestry. Meroplanktonic larvae and other aquatic life may land in “Other”. Fungi, molluscs, arachnids and the rest also fall under “Other”.
